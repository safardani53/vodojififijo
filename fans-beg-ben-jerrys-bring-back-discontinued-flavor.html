    THE scoop is that Ben and Jerry's discontinued their Crème Brûlée ice cream flavor and fans are begging for its return.

    Ben and Jerry's has kept its spot as a top contender amongst America's ice cream brands.

    Customers have been graced with exciting ice cream flavors created by the Vermont-based ice cream company since 1978.

    Some fans have claimed their favorites, and they refuse to live without them.

    Take the Crème Brûlée flavor for example.

    The flavor of the decadent dessert is described as, "creamy" and "sweet".

    Read more in Ben and Jerry's

    "You’re able to detect some notes of caramel flavor but the thing I liked most about the swirl was that it adds a unique texture to the pint," a fan wrote in a product review on OnSecondScoop.com.

    "It breaks easily on your spoon but has a crisp crunch when you bite into it."

    The creaminess of the ice cream is said to be reminiscent of the custard used in the actual baked dessert.

    Ice cream fanatics expressed their disappointment in the discontinuation of the Crème Brûlée flavor in the comment section below the fan's review.

    Most read in Money

    "This stuff is awesome and one of my favorite from Ben and Jerry's! Are they seriously burying this flavor in the graveyard?! No!" one person commented.

    Another added: "What???? No more crème brûlée??? Why are such yummy flavors put in ice cream archives???

    "Will the old flavors ever be brought back??? Ben and Jerry's ice creams cannot be duplicated. Can you say withdraws???"

    "This is one of the best ice cream flavors ever! The creaminess of the custard is amazing! The custard alone would be a great flavor," someone else wrote.

    "When you bite into the sugar topping...it's like eating an actual crème brûlée!

    "How they were able to keep the crunch of the burnt sugar is amazing! I'm in Austin, TX. If anyone knows of a store with this flavor, please share!!! I will buy every pint!" they continued.

    Others took to Twitter to plead for the Crème Brûlée's return.

    "Ben and Jerry's - I pay extra to buy your ice cream because of your social justice platform. Thank you for your statement. Please bring back crème brulee," a Twitter user said.

    In response, a person wrote: "Seconding both the thanks and the call for creme brulee to return."

    The Crème Brûlée flavor now rests in the "Flavor Graveyard" on the Ben and Jerry's website with the years 2007-2012 plastered on its tomb.

    This isn't the first time that the beloved ice cream brand took a fan favorite off the shelves.

    The limited edition One Love flavor that was inspired by Bob Marley was last available in 2018.

    The brand's Peanuts! Popcorn! was also short-lived.
