    Sainsbury's recalls Alpro Growing Up soya milk amid fears the drink has not been properly sterilised

    SAINSBURY'S has recalled a follow-on milk for babies and toddlers amid fears the drink has not been properly sterilised. The supermarket is warning customers not to drink the one litre pack of Alpro Growing Up Drink for one to three-year-olds with a best before date of March 5 2018.

    24 Jun 2024, 00:00

    SAINSBURY'S has recalled a follow-on milk for babies and toddlers amid fears the drink has not been properly sterilised.

    The supermarket is warning customers not to drink the one litre pack of Alpro Growing Up Drink for one to three-year-olds with a best before date of March 5 2018.

    Sainsbury's states on its website: "Alpro have brought to our attention that through routine testing they have detected a quality issue with the above product which could cause the product to curdle, and therefore taste sour when opened.

    "As a precautionary measure, we're asking all customers who have bought this product not to consume or use it, and to return it to their nearest Sainsbury’s store, where they will receive a full refund.

    "No other Alpro products or date codes are affected by this issue, and we apologise for the inconvenience this has caused."

    The Food Standards Agency added: "If you have purchased any of the above product do not drink it. Instead, return it to the store from where it was purchased for a full refund."

    Sainsbury's recently announced it is to launch a new heat and temperature sensitive label that will change colour when food is off.

    The “Smart Fresh” label changes colour from yellow to purple to show how long a pack has been open.

    This acts as a reminder for reducing the amount of perfectly good to eat product being thrown away ‘just in case’.

    The clever new label is also temperature sensitive, with the rate of colour change designed to slow down the cooler the fridge is.

    MOST READ IN NEWS

    For now, Sainsbury’s is testing the new technology on its own brand cooked ham only e across all its UK stores.

    But the retailer confirmed to the Sun Online that it might roll it out across other products if the consumer response to the new pack is positive.

    The retailer introduced the label as new figures from Wrap, the government’s waste advisory body, revealed that UK households are throwing away 1.9 millions slices of ham every day at a cost of over £170m every year.

    The new Smart Fresh ham packaging is the latest in a string of initiative as Sainsbury’s ramps up in efforts to combat food waste.

    It is also part of supermarket’s £10m “Waste less, Save more initiative”.
