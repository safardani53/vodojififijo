    THE VIEW host Whoopi Goldberg has opened up about her health struggles in her new book.

    She warned readers to not fool around when it comes to taking care of themselves.

    Whoopi's book, Two Old Broads: Stuff You Need to Know That You Didn't Know You Needed to Know, which she wrote with Dr M.E. Hecht, was released on Tuesday.

    In it, she recounted many stories in her life and got candid about her recent health struggles.

    "I had no idea how many annoying little things would start to happen to my body as I aged," the View host wrote.

    The 66-year-old went on to say that when she stands, her knees make so much noise that it sounds like they are playing clackers.

    Whoopi wrote that she has never ached in her life as much as she does now and therefore, knows when it is going to rain.

    At one point, Whoopi thought she "left her coccyx on the chair" after getting up.

    "I think, Did I just break something? Is something wrong here? Should I be sounding like this? Apparently, yes, I should," she explained.

    Whoopi then told readers that she has a "touch of Arthritis."

    "Moving my neck around, moving my shoulders, I have bursitis. I mean, I have a lot of ‘itises.’”

    The host then went on to explain that a few years ago she had a bad cough that lasted nearly a year, so she finally went to the doctors.

    "And the day of You Know Who’s second State of the Union address, I started thinking, Boy, I really don’t feel good, and this can’t just be because I don’t like him. So, I called my trusty sidekick, who was in the city, and said, ‘Hey, I’m not feeling good,'" she wrote.

    Whoopi then explained, after an ambulance was called, that she was being kept in the hospital and was told she had pneumonia in both of her lungs and was septic, which is a possibly life-threatening condition that results from the body's response to an infection.

    The host had her lungs drained and spent a month in the hospital.

    WHOOPI'S ADVICE

    She then offered advice for her readers about taking care of their health.

    “So, if you’re not feeling well, don’t fool around. Had I reached out to my friends or a doctor about that cough after two or three months, I might have saved myself the year and a half recovery.”

    Whoopi also wrote that sometimes your body tells on you, but you have to listen and take care of it.

    "Your body is your best friend. It tells you everything, so listen to it."

    And although Whoopi's symptoms were pretty clear-cut, she also warned her readers that it is always good to get a second opinion.

    'BE RIDICULOUS'

    In another chapter, Whoopi opened up about why she "acts ridiculous" sometimes."

    The actress also explained the 24/30 rule that helps her live a more positive life.

    "There's enough time - there's twenty-four hours for goodness' sake -for you to get all angry at whoever or whatever," she wrote.

    "But you gotta take at least half an hour to just be ridiculous inside your own mind. Be as ridiculous as you want to be."

    Whoopi continued: "Listen, there is enough stress to last you day in and day out, so why not say, 'You know what? I'm going to fart every twenty feet I go.'"

    "Say it in your head and if it makes you laugh, that's a good thing," she ended.

    WHOOPI THE AUTHOR

    Two Old Broads is not the first book that Whoopi has written.

    She has written fictional children's books called the Sugar Plum Ballerinas series.
