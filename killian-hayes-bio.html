    Early Life

    Killian Hayes was born Killian Deron Antron Hayes on July 27, 2001, in Lakeland, Florida, United States, to his professional basketball player father DeRon Hayes, and mother, Sandrine Hayes.

    Information related to his siblings is currently unavailable. Born in the States, Killian moved to France because of his father’s career.

    Interestingly, Killian was born in the same hospital as his father DeRon, who was playing in the ABA at the time.

    After his father played in the LNB Pro A, the French top league Hayes began living in Cholet.

    His father is American, while his mother is a native of Cholet, France.

    Sandrine is also a former basketball player. She met DeRon because of her sister, who was the girlfriend of DeRon’s teammate.

    As a kid, Killian idolized professional basketball player Dwyane Wade and used to watch highlights of games on YouTube.

    Killian then began playing for Chlolet Basket at the youth level.

    He always wanted to play high school and collegiate basketball in the States, but his father advised him to remain in France.

    In 2016, he began playing for Espoirs Cholet in LNB Espoirs after turning down the opportunity to join a prestigious sports institute in Paris, INSEP, upon his father’s advice.

    Career

    Killian Hayes played for the Cholet from 2017 to 2019, making his debut with the senior team on October 21, 2017. He was just 16 years, 2 months, and 24 days.

    He signed a three-year contract with the team before the 2018-19 season after considering various other European clubs.

    However, he later signed a three-year contract with the Basketball Bundesliga team ratiopharm Ulm on August 2, 2019.

    Basketball Bundesliga (BBL) is the top league in Germany and the EuroCup.

    His NBA dreams came true when Detroit Pistons picked him 7th overall in the 2020 NBA Draft. He signed a rookie contract with the team on December 1, 2020.

    Altercation & Suspension

    The Pistons point guard Killian Hayes was hit with the harshest punishment for the altercation between players on the Magic and Pistons on Wednesday night game on December 28, 2022.

    It all happened just before halftime of the contest between the Magic and the Pistons. With only 30 seconds remaining, Wagner and Hayes chased a loose ball in the backcourt.

    As the ball was about to roll out of play, Franz Wagner shoved Killian in the back, sending him tumbling into the bench.

    Then Killian popped up and punched Wagner in the back of the head.

    All in all, 11 players were suspended following the incident; nine from Orlando and two from the Pistons.

    Hayes was suspended for three games without pay, and he forfeited $120,781 for his role in on-court alteration.
