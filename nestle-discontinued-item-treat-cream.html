    A POPULAR Nestle product has been axed, leaving devout fans devastated.

    The brand is one of the most popular ones customers see at the grocery store.

    The Switzerland-based company makes everything from drinks to snacks.

    But this time, it has some bad news about the Carnation Extra Thick Cream, which has been used by an inquiring fans' family for "3 generations."

    "It was a difficult decision for us because we are proud of all our products and do not like to disappoint our consumers," Nestle's customer service care team said in a response to a customer on Twitter.

    The product was made with sterilized cream and sodium citrate.

    Walmart's description said it could be used as a "delicious topping" for berries, fruit, or dessert.

    However, it does not recommend using the product for coffee or tea.

    Online, the Carnation cream was rated 3.2 stars out of five by customers.

    This included two five-star ratings out of a total of five reviews.

    OTHER ALTERNATIVES

    While the heavy cream is gone, Netsle fans can still find other options at the grocery store.

    This includes the Media Crema Table Cream, which sells for $2.18 in a 7.6 fluid-ounce can.

    It also makes a lite version at the same price and size.

    They can both be used for a number of different foods including fruits, sweets, and even entrees such as soups and pasta, according to the product details.

    Plus, if you're looking to give your coffee or tea an extra kick – then there is always its Coffee mate brand.

    It makes several flavors from French Vanilla to Cinnamon Vanilla Creme.

    Coffee mate 32 fluid ounce containers sell for $4.26. at Walmart.

    MORE DISCONTINUED ITEMS

    Speaking of coffee creamers, Nestle might have some bad news for customers.

    That's because the company has confirmed that its Chocolate Raspberry flavor is gone.

    It's actually been gone before 2012 – but fans have been begging for it to come back.

    Another brand, Gatorade, has axed its Blueberry Pomegranate flavor.

    The flavor was described on the label as a "low-calorie thirst quencher" option, which is what all of its G2 varieties aim to bring to the table.

    Its ingredients consisted of artificial flavoring and coloring, as well as sugar and sucralose.

    Coca-Cola said that it has stopped producing its orange vanilla soda.

    The company offered a sugar-free variety of this flavor as well.

    “Coca-Cola Orange Vanilla Zero Sugar was discontinued so that we can focus on flavors that show the most potential for growth and scale," the brand said in response to a fan that asked about the orange vanilla soda.

    And Cheerios recently said that it had discontinued its maple flavor.

    Along with maple syrup, the cereal contained whole-grain oats, sugar, brown sugar syrup, and natural flavors.

    It came with nine total sugars per serving.
