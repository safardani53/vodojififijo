    'Larger than life' hotel manager escorts the Queen away from dinner after intruder bursts in on her

    14 Sep 2024, 00:00

    A LARGER than life hotel manager escorted the Queen from lunch after an intruder allegedly burst in and sparked a royal security scare.

    David Morgan-Hewitt, managing director of the Goring Hotel in London's Belgravia, ensured Her Majesty's safety as she walked back to her car after an intruder reportedly sprinted into the hotel.

    Mr Morgan-Hewitt was pictured leading the monarch away from the hotel yesterday lunchtime.

    He shook her hand and waved as her car departed.

    A man had sprinted through the front door while the Queen was dining, the Mail Online reported.

    Minutes later he was wrestled out of the hotel by a man believed to be royal protection.

    related stories

    The 90-year-old monarch had been attending a private function with senior servants when the man allegedly dashed into the hotel at about 1.30pm.

    The protection officer reportedly took the man's details and patted him down before letting him go.

    The alleged intruder was spotted on the District line before he entered the hotel.

    A witness told Mail Online he appeared to be drunk and was shouting: "I'm Irish, I'm hard as f***."

    The five-star Goring Hotel, close to Buckingham Palace, is a known haunt of the Royal Family.

    Its website says Mr Morgan-Hewitt is "as much of an institution as the hotel itself".

    The hotel has a Royal Warrant of appointment to HM The Queen for Hospitality Services, issued to tradesmen who are regular suppliers of goods and services to the royal households.

    The Middleton family stayed at the hotel the night before the marriage of the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge.

    The Queen is reportedly a good host herself, spending £1.4MILLION year for her 96,000 guests – including £500k on BOOZE.
