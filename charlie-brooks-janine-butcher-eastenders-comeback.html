    EASTENDERS legend Charlie Brooks says she could yet return to Albert Square to play fan favourite villain Janine Butcher.
    The 37-year-old actress has appeared on the long running BBC One soap on and off since 1999 and has featured in some of the show’s most iconic and high flying storylines.

    Janine was last seen on screen in 2014 as the character headed off to Paris to start a new life after years of drama in Albert Square.
    The door has been left wide open for Janine to return and now actress Charlie is teasing that she would be well up for returning to her iconic role.
    “I look back on my time in EastEnders really fondly. I had a great time,” she said, opening up to Woman magazine.
    “I was very fortunate with the character, and got so many wonderful and different storylines, so it was really fun to play,” she continued.

    Reflecting further, Charlie says she would be open to returning to the show.
    “I still speak to some of the cast and I’d never say never to returning,” she teased.
    “I’ve no idea where Janine’s been or what she’s been up to,” she added.

    EastEnders supervillain Janine Butcher - played by Charlie Brooks - pushes Barry off a cliff

    Janine is perhaps most famous for killing off Barry Evans in a shock storyline that saw her push the car salesman off a hill in Scotland.
    The episode aired on New Year’s Day in 2004 – but Janine escaped the law to get away with the murder.
    However she was later sent to jail after the death of Laura Beale.

    The two women had been fighting and although Laura’s death was actually an accident (she fell down a flight of stairs), Pat Butcher refused to give Janine an alibi despite being with her at the time of Laura’s death.
    Police found Janine’s DNA on Laura after she died and a post mortem revealed she had been in a fight before her death – and Pat’s silence sealed Janine’s fate.
    She returned to the Square in 2008 – leaving again in 2014 as it was hinted she planned to con millionaires abroad.
