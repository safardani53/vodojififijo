    LITTLE People's Isabel Roloff Rock has shared a cryptic quote on Instagram about "never tolerating behaviors."

    The post comes after Matt and Zach's feud about selling the family farm.

    Isabel originally shared a post about letting people see the consequences of their actions.

    She went on to say that her mother warned her, but she didn't listen.

    Many of her followers reposted it and it got a lot of attention.

    Then, the 26-year-old shared someone else's quote to her story with the caption, "Add this to my last post."

    "Having compassion and empathy for why someone behaves the way they do based on their experiences, never means you have to tolerate the behavior or hold the space for it," it read.

    "You can absolutely have compassion and set a boundary. This is what it means to also have compassion for yourself," the post ended.

    Just a few days ago, Isabel posted a cryptic meme on her stories.

    It was from the Will Ferrell movie, The Campaign, and read, "Bring your brooms... cause it's a mess."

    Isabel has not publicly come out and said it is about the family's feud or whose side she is on.

    FAMILY FEUD

    Isabel's father-in-law Matt, 60, explained why he was putting the "Roloff farm up for sale" in a lengthy Instagram post. 

    "My ultimate hope was that the entire Roloff Farms property would stay in our family for generations to come," the post read.

    The Roloff patriarch claimed his "twin boys" could not work together "toward a possible joint sale" and had to move on to other interests.

    He mentioned that his sons Jeremy and Zach, both 32, had "decided individually that the timing was not right for them to purchase a part of the farm at this time."

    In the comments section, Zach accused his father of "manipulating the narrative right before" season 23 premieres on TLC on May 17th.

    Zach argued that Matt was not "taking responsibility for his own actions and blaming others."

    He slammed his father's "new shocking low of cowardice and manipulation of his family and kids for his own gain."

    During the recent trailer, Zach and Matt were at odds over who would take over the farm.

    SELLING THE FARM

    After the fight settled, Matt decided to put the Oregon pumpkin patch up for sale.

    About 16 of 109 acres have gone up for sale.

    The reality star mentioned that the sale is "a tiny bit scary but mostly exciting."

    "It's time to move toward the next season of life," Matt concluded.

    According to the Wall Street Journal, the Roloff patriarch has asked for about $4million with the sale of the "original family home and bright red barn."

    He talked to WSJ about the sale and told them that it is getting harder now that he is 60.

    Matt wants to retire to Arizona with his girlfriend, Caryn, and therefore, can't run the farm anymore.
