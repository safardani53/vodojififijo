    29 Aug 2024, 00:00

    WHILE the ease and convenience of an automatic car wash is always tempting, nothing can beat the satisfaction of a jet washing your own pride and joy.

    The best pressure washers can transform your car in minutes, and there’s no risk of scratched paintwork or damaged wing mirrors.

    As hand-finished car wash locations, with teams of people armed with microfibre cloths, pop up all over, it’s more than tempting to chuck £15 a week to get the car cleaned.

    However, by our calculations, if done every two weeks that’s a whopping £375 a year — allowing for two weeks non-wash — per car.

    So perhaps it’s time to invest in your own jet wash; round up the kids and put the fun back into washing the family motor.

    And for far less than £375, you can have something that reaches to the roof, lifts off all the grime and preps the bodywork adequately for a good polish, without risking any scratches.

    You could be forgiven for believing Kärcher is the overlord when it comes to blasting off with a pressure washer, but there are some other top brands out there fighting grime just as well.

    Whether you're looking to clean your car or your patio, here are some of the best pressure washers for 2021.

    1. Kärcher K4 Power Control

    • Kärcher K4 Power Control, £199.99, from Halfords - buy here

    The Kärcher K4 Power Control always gets outstanding reviews across the industry.

    If you have plenty of patio and other cleaning tasks involving the jet wash, you’re not just saving on car washing by investing in a K4.

    With Kärcher’s 3-year guarantee, this highly durable pressure washer comes with two lances and advises medium setting for cars. That’s some power washer, right there.

    The "Plug and Clean" system allows detergents to be put through the unit, enabling you to use your favourite car shampoo.

    What’s more, a six-metre high pressure hose length ensures all cars can be cleaned thoroughly.

    2. Makita HW111

    • Makita HW111, £107.62, from ToolStore Uk - buy here

    The Makita HW111 is lighter and easier to use than previous designs.

    Though the flow rate isn’t one of the highest, the Makita has scored well in tests.

    With a 3-year Makita guarantee, this pressure cleaner features an armed hose, built in accessories holder and wind-in hose reel, so storage is neat and tidy, and the hose is less likely to kink and frustrate the task at hand.

    The instruction manual is diagrammatical, showing how you can connect detergent to get a great shine on your motor.

    3. GMC GPW165

    • GMC GPW165, £206.10, from Amazon - buy here

    An award-winning design, the GMC GPW165 is one of the pricier options on our list.

    It’s hefty at 20kg, but this makes it sturdy when the eight-metre hose is at full length.

    With good flow rate and pressure (165 bar), the soap bottle, rotary brush, fix brush lance and turbo nozzle will help get your motor sparkling without too much elbow grease.

    There’s brass nozzles for fixing the hose to the tap, which are more robust than the usual plastic offered and the ten-metre electric cord is more than generous.

    4. Kärcher K2 Power Control

    • Kärcher K2 Power Control, £139.99, from Halfords - buy here

    Costing just a fraction of the yearly price of outsourcing the weekly car wash, the K2 features an extendable handle to access the roof and keep your distance from the water spray, a trigger gun and Full Control and Dirt Blaster lances.

    The unit has wheels for easy movement around the car.

    What’s more, there’s a car kit, including a wash brush, foam nozzle and car shampoo — offering maximum value-for-money by helping you to get into those tough places around the wheels.

    The durable plastic casing and high quality components are guaranteed for two years.

    5. Spear & Jackson Pressure Washer

    • Spear and Jackson Pressure Washer, £160, from Argos - buy here

    Prefer to hand soap the bodywork and rims?

    Coming with a three-year warranty, this Spear & Jackson is a favourite with consumers.

    For a reasonable price, this washer comes with a range of attachments, making it versatile for other jobs too.

    The 2000W features an adjustable lance that allows you to alter the level of water pressure to suit the task, so you can be tougher on wheels and tyres and more gentle on car panels.

    It doesn’t however, have an attachable sno-foam lance, so a little bit more effort is needed before you get spraying.

    6. Bosch Fontus Portable Pressure Washer

    • Bosch Fontus Portable Pressure Washer, £269.95, from Amazon - buy here

    This cordless pressure washer from Bosch is best if mobility is important.

    The 15L tank holds enough water to clean three muddy bikes, and it boasts 3 pressure modes and 4 spray patterns.

    One happy customer said: "I originally bought this to help with washing my motorcycle however I found that it's very useful for so many things around the house and garden. There are three different pressure settings, the lowest is a light dribble which is great to wash off muddy paws or to lightly water plants with."

    7. Blaupunkt Compact Pressure Washer

    • Blaupunkt Compact Pressure Washer, £74.99, from Amazon - buy here

    Incredibly small and lightweight, this Blaupunkt pressure washer is ideal if size is a concern.

    It weighs in at only 4.2kg, making it easy to carry for the whole family, while its small size ensures you can easily store it away in the shed or even the boot of your car. And with various modes including a high and low pressure setting, it still packs a punch.

    It also includes a detergent dispenser gun, perfect for adding soap of car shampoo prying to cleaning.

    8. Ryobi Pressure Washer

    • Ryobi Pressure Washer, £111.99, from Amazon - buy here

    A great mid-price option, this Ryobi pressure washer has surprisingly high performance making it excellent value for money.

    Its maximum pressure of 130 bar combines with a max flow of 380L/H, which is plenty enough to blast away even the most stubborn dirt and grime.

    Coming equipped with multiple wands including a handy brush, it’s all the pressure washer you need for a professional looking home wash. The 1L detergent tank is large enough for multiple washes, while the compact design and easy access, steel-armed hose will make those weekly cleans a breeze.

    What is the best pressure washer to buy for home use?

    The best pressure washer to buy for home use will depend on your individual needs.

    For someone who is at entry level on heavy duty jobs, we recommend looking at Kärcher K4 Power Control.

    If you are looking to get a power washer for light duty cleaning jobs, take a look at this Kärcher model instead.

    What is a good PSI for a pressure washer?

    For washing cars, you typically only need a pressure washer with maximum of 2000 psi. In reality, you’ll be able to thoroughly clean your car with a pressure of as low as 1100 psi, so if you’re on a budget then keep this in mind.

    Of course, it doesn’t hurt to have a higher psi, so if money allows, go big. Typically, the higher the psi, the more money it will cost.

    Which brand of pressure washer is best?

    It’s no secret that Kärcher dominates the higher end pressure washer market. The German brand prides itself on innovation, and produces a wide range of top tier pressure washers for virtually all needs.

    In short, you can’t go wrong by investing in one of its award-winning designs. Look out for other large reputable names though. Rival German brand Bosch is noted for its expert manufacturing and easy to use designs, which rival Karcher for their long lasting quality.
