    ASHTON Kutcher has admitted to being angry after his ex-wife aired their dirty laundry in her 2019 tell-all book, Inside Out.

    The actor opened up about his feelings on the past relationship, and the experience of becoming a stepfather to three pre-teens at the age of 26, during a new interview with Esquire magazine.

    Ashton, 44, revealed that he was "f**king pissed" after Demi, 60, decided to disclose their past marital issues to the world within the pages of her memoir.

    The That 70's Show star disclosed that it wasn't due to being embarrassed that he and the G.I. Jane actress's marriage had problems but because he had finally reached a place of normalcy within his current family life.

    He explained: "I'd finally gotten to a place where the press had really laid off me and Mila, and my life and my family. And then the next day, [the paparazzi] are at my kids’ school.” 

    Before allowing that anger to resurface any further, The Ranch actor took on a "no hard feelings" attitude and admitted he didn't "want to open anything up in that realm."

    READ MORE ON ASHTON KUTCHER

    Within the interview, Ashton reflected on how his "life changed" after the news broke regarding his relationship with the Ghost star with much of the attention revolving around the 15-year age gap between them.

    He also touched upon just how much he had to adapt after he became stepfather to Demi and actor Bruce Willis's daughters; Rumer, 34, Scout, 31, and Tallulah, 28, after the pair married.

    The A-Grade Investments co-founder stated: "I was twenty-six, bearing the responsibility of an eight-year-old, a ten-year-old, and a twelve-year-old," and reasoned that it must be "how some teen parents must experience their twenties."

    Ashton briefly spoke about his ex-wife's miscarriage, as he explained: "Losing a kid that you think you're going to have, and that close to thinking you're going to have a kid, is really, really painful."

    The actor continued: "I love kids," reasoning, "I wouldn't have gotten married to a woman that had three kids if I didn't love kids.

    "The idea of having another kid would have been incredible."

    The No Strings Attached star seemed to have come to terms with the loss as he revealed: "For whatever reason, I had to have that experience."

    As for the ending of his first marriage, the 44-year-old admitted to feeling like a "failure."

    Ashton divulged: "Nothing makes you feel like a failure like divorce," before adding, "Divorce feels like a wholesale f**king failure.

    "You failed at marriage."

    The interview wasn't all about past relationships, as the SAG award-nominated actor made took time to dote about his current wife, Mila Kunis, 39.

    The couple, who met while they both starred on That 70's Show together, married in 2015 and share children Wyatt, eight, and Dimitri, six.

    Ashton explained: "The thing about Mila that made our relationship accelerate was that I had always admired her."

    He continued: "Her talents, her skills, her gifts. But I knew that she didn’t need me. And she knew that I didn’t need her."

    Ashton revealed that it also helped that the couple "already knew all of each other’s dirt."

    The That '90s Show actor also stated that it doesn't hurt that his "wife is so much cooler" than he is.

    RELATIONSHIP HISTORY

    Ashton has had his fair share of leading ladies and love interests.

    The Dude Where's My Car actor dated American actress Monet Mazur, who is famously known for her role in All American, from 1995 to 1996, when they were both teenagers.

    He was also attached to actress and model January Jones, known for playing Betty Draper in the hit television series Mad Men, from 1998 to 2001.

    Regarding their relationship, Kutcher's future ex-wife Demi Moore recalled what led to the Vengeance star's split with January.

    In her autobiography Inside Out, the Striptease star remembered Kutcher and January's relationship fell apart after rumors surfaced about January's supposed affair with her Bandits co-star Bruce Willis.

    Demi claimed: "Ashton was convinced [January and Bruce] had a fling on set."

    When Demi asked January about her rumored fling with her ex-husband Bruce, the latter responded: "Are you serious? I told him a hundred times, I didn't want to f**k that old man."

    Following Kutcher's split from Jones, he pursued a relationship with 22-year-old fashion student Ashley Ellerin.

    However, the two never made it on their date because she was murdered in February 2001 by the Hollywood Ripper, Michael Gargiulo.

    During Gargiulo's trial in 2019, Kutcher testified that he “was trying to take her on a date” to a Grammy Awards afterparty, and after not hearing back from her, he assumed he arrived "too late and she [had already] left," but her body was found the next morning.

    Gargiulo was found guilty of Ellerin's murder and sentenced to death in July 2021.

    American actress and model Ashley Scott is known for her work in the video games The Last of Us and The Last of Us Part II, where she voices the character Maria Miller.

    In 2001, Scott and Kutcher started a relationship but it only lasted for around nine months.

    Brittany Murphy was an American actress and singer who famously starred as Tai Frasier in Clueless.

    Kutcher and Murphy started dating after filming the movie Just Married and had sparked engagement rumors in 2003, but the couple eventually parted ways.

    Murphy then passed away in December 2009 at the age of 32 following a battle with pneumonia.

    “She was like the person who walked in the room and she was always the first person to dance,” Kutcher said of his ex on Nightline in May 2010.

    “You know when you go to a party and everybody is standing around, and the music is going - and you know sooner or later everyone is going to dance.

    "She was always the first person to dance. And I just try to connect to that and celebrate who she was and how she was and not make sense of it 'cause there is no sense of making sense of it.” 

    ASHTON'S MARRIAGES

    On September 24, 2005, Ashton and Demi tied the knot in a private wedding ceremony at their Beverly Hills home.

    The pair began dating sometime in 2003, three years after Demi divorced her first husband, Bruce Willis.

    On November 17, 2011, Ashton and Demi revealed they were separating.

    In a statement, the former Punk'd host announced: "I will forever cherish the time I spent with Demi.

    "Marriage is one of the most difficult things in the world and unfortunately sometimes they fail."

    On November 26, 2013, the former couple's divorce was finalized.

    On July 4, 2015, Ashton walked down the aisle with fellow That '70s Show alum Mila Kunis.

    The pair held a private wedding ceremony at Parris Ranch in Oak Glen, California.

    At that point, Ashton and Mila had already been dating for three years.

    The co-stars struck up a romance after reuniting at a party at Ashton's house in January 2012.

    As previously mentioned, they met several years before while co-starring on the popular sitcom when Mila was 14 years old and Ashton was 19.
