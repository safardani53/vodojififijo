    Granger Smith began teaching himself to play the guitar at the age of 14, and he has obsessed with music ever since. He recorded his first album as a freshman student at Texas A&M, which caught the eye of EMI Music Publishing! The company offered Granger a songwriting contract, which the college student signed and prompted him to move to Nashville, Tennessee a year later. However, Granger forged his own way in the country music scene. He eventually made the move back to Texas, and continued putting out multiple albums (he has 11 total as of 2019) and music videos. 

    Although Granger had already released multiple albums (‘Dirt Road Driveway’ especially had commercial success), he landed an official record deal in 2015. Overall, Granger has enjoyed immense success on music charts and among his ever growing fan base. His most famous hit, ‘Backroad Song,’ even peaked at No. 1 on Billboard’s ‘Country Airplay’ chart. The country singer married Amber Bartlett in 2010, whom he met the year prior on the music video set for his song ‘Don’t Listen To The Radio.’

    Granger and Amber welcomed three children together — River, Lincoln and London — but sadly, River passed away in June 2019. The child was just three years old, and the youngest in the family. He reportedly died in a ‘drowning accident.’
