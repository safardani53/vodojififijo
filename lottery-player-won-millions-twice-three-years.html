    26 Feb 2024, 00:00

    A LOTTERY player scooped $10million twice in the last three years, defying odds of at least 3.3million to one.

    Juan Hernandez, from Uniondale, New York, bought the Deluxe scratch-off ticket from a store in Hempstead.

    He then realized he had pocketed the $10million top prize.

    The odds of winning on the Deluxe card is one in 3,521,600.00, according to the New York Lottery.

    Hernandez received $6,510,000 in a lump sum following deductions.

    His big win came after he won $10million on a Cash Spectacular ticket in 2019.

    The game offers more than $350million in prizes and the odds of winning the jackpot is one in 3,312,480, according to lottery officials.

    Hernandez said he’s trying to still spend the money from his first big win, the NY Lottery revealed.

    The punter’s stunning victory is just the latest success to hit the headlines in recent weeks.

    Ana Maya, of North Carolina, found a $532,234 winning ticket wedged in an old notebook – just weeks before it was due to expire.

    She told the North Carolina Education Lottery that she had lost the ticket while moving to a new place.

    She started panicking when she was unpacking and realized the ticket was nowhere to be found.

    Maya said: "These last couple of months have been very stressful trying to find it," the winner said. "I totally forgot where I put it.”

    She then found it pressed between the pages of an old school notebook.

    The 32-year-old said she planned to spend her winnings on a vacation and intends to put some aside for her kids.

    STUNNING WINS

    Winners have 180 days to claim their prize before it expires; Maya claimed hers before it would have expired on March 6.

    And retired teacher Louis Paine Jr, 77, scooped $110,000 after using a combination of his children’s birthdays as his winning numbers.

    The 77-year-old was left “flabbergasted” after learning he had won the prize after buying a Cash 5 drawing in North Carolina on February 1.

    He told the North Carolina Education Lottery: “Of course, I didn’t believe it.”

    Louis spent 40 years as an educator and said that he enjoyed playing the lottery as it raises money for local schools.

    He said: “Giving back to education is very important. It’s the greatest thing we have in our country.”

    The Sun previously reported that an army vet won $4million on the lottery after he used the numbers from a fortune cookie.

    Gabriel Fierro, 60, bought the sweet treat while he was dining with his wife at the Red Bowl Asian Bistro in Charlotte, North Carolina.
